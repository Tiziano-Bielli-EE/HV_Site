CURRENT TRANSFORMERS ACCURACY:

Current transformers are devices used to scale down currents that otherwise would be to high to measure. In this page I'll show the results of some performance tests that I did over some current transformers.

TEST PARAMETERS:

When chosing an AC current transformer accuracy is only one of the many parameter of interest. Depending on your application you might consider:

  • Nominal currents
  • Ratio
  • Accuracy/Linearity
  • Phase angle error: when measuring energy is important to take in account, and eventually compensate, the phase error of CTs.
  • Saturation Voltage / Current: every CT, depending on the burden resistor, will be able to transduce accurately currents up to a maximum point where the error will increase due to saturation.
  • DC tolerance: it's known that transformers don't work with DC but, during normal usage, there could be a DC component (coming from single diode rectifier and other electronics). If the transformer is not DC tolerant it will saturate with a consequent reduction in accuracy.
  • Openable CT
  • Mechanical size
  • Other solutions (Shunt, Hall effect sensors)
  • Price
  • ...
